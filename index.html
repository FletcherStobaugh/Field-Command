AN INDIE RTS IN DEVELOPMENT

Small-unit modern combat. 15 minutes. Your call.

Four asymmetric factions. Named operators with XP and gear. Base building, hero micro, and the kind of explosive teamfights RTS forgot how to make. Think Warcraft 3 took a deployment to the modern world.

We'll email when the Steam page goes live. That's it.

FOUR FACTIONS. FOUR GAMES.

  • Coalition — slow to deploy, devastating when set. Drones, air strikes, JDAMs.
  • Bloc — mass and artillery. Trade quality for volume. Artillery from offscreen.
  • Cartel — hide and swarm. Technicals, IEDs, mobile spawns. No traditional base.
  • Contractors — no tech tree. Buy any unit from any faction. Toolbox faction.

NAMED OPERATORS.

  • Viper — recon sniper. Marks targets, precision volleys.
  • Wrench — combat engineer. Claymores, repairs, dropped bunkers.
  • Ghost — drone operator. Reveals fog, hacks units, suicide swarms.
  • Preacher — political officer. Rallies morale, levies militia.

WHY THIS EXISTS.

Warcraft 3 launched in 2002. Twenty-three years of attempts to build a successor, and every one focused on fantasy or sci-fi. No one has given the WC3 multiplayer crowd a modern military game with the same small-unit feel, hero micro, and asymmetric factions. That's the gap.

Field Command isn't trying to be realistic. It's trying to be fun. Arcade HP. Voice-line personality. 15-minute matches. Chaos.
